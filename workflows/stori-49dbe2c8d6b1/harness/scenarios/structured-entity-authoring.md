# Structured Entity Authoring

## Why This Scenario Exists

Stori wins by making structured canon easier than ad hoc notes. Entity creation must feel fast, clear, and intentionally constrained.

## Representative User

A creator is adding a new faction, character, or location after the initial world setup and wants the right schema without designing it from scratch.

## Task

Create a new structured entity, fill in its key fields, and save it as part of the trusted canon.

## Expected Outcome

- the creator can choose from predefined entity types quickly
- required identity fields are obvious
- the form encourages structured canon instead of a generic blank document
- the saved entity is immediately browsable and ready for linking

## Validation Ideas

- create one entity from scratch and one from a lightweight stub
- verify required fields, summaries, and relationship slots are visible
- confirm the saved entity appears in browse and search surfaces

## Failure Signals

- the authoring UI feels as open-ended as a plain notes app
- entity types are unclear or inconsistent
- saved entities are hard to find or do not feel canonical
